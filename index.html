    About Neel Fary

  • Location
    • It is located in Azad Jammu Kashmir AJK. Neel Fairy Meadows are found in Forward Kahuta, District Haveli in Azad Kashmir. Forward Kahuta is a beautiful village, located 250 kilometres (155 miles) away from Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan. Formerly it was a part of District Bagh but it was the July 2009 when it became a separate District, of the State of Azad Kashmir.


  • Weather
    • Neel Fairy Lake is famous for its stunning beauty that captivates tourists with its crystal clear water, surrounding lush green area, tranquil ambiance, and heavy mountains. One of the exceptional characteristics of Neel Fairy Lake is its pristine water. The lake is fed by melting glaciers, ensuring that the water remains pure and unpolluted and this makes it the perfect tourist destination spot. Tourists can witness their own reflection in the mirror-like surface of the lake, creating a sense of serenity and harmony with nature.

    About Ratti Gali

  • Location
    • Ratti Gali Lake is an alpine glacial lake which is located in Neelum Valley, Azad Kashmir, Pakistan . The lake is located at an altitude of 3,683 metres (12,083 ft). The lake is fed by the surrounding glacier waters of the mountains.


  • Weather
    • The weather conditions in this area are highly changeable, with each season bringing in a fresh influx of colour.

    The spring season extends from March to May, while the summer season stretches from May to August. Summers see the bulk of tourist activity in the area, as it is primed for walking, hiking or just simply basking in the sun.

    As the temperature dips (it can drop as low as 0 degrees Celsius), the crowds thin significantly. The lake remains completely frozen from November to April. As the weather can take a dodgy turn, be sure to pack multiple layers of warm clothes.

    About Peer Chinasi

  • Location
    • Pir Chinasi (also spelled as Peer Chinasi) is a shrine and a tourist destination located about 30 kilometers (19 mi) east of Muzaffarabad, the capital city of Azad Kashmir administered by Pakistan.[1] It is located on the top of hills at the height of 2,900 metres (9,500 ft). The mountain peak has gained large fame for its ziyarat of a famous saint, Sayed Hussain Shah Bukhari.


  • Weather
    • The months from October to March are the coldest ones in Pir Chinasi. Pir Chinasi in winters is covered with heavy and thick snow during the mentioned period. Contrarily, the temperature in May and June are 20 and 23 degrees centigrade respectively, making the summer season an ideal time in Pir Chinasi weather to view the green landscapes.

    The pine trees at Pir Chinasi roads and surrounding areas are a natural shield when it comes to overcoming pollution. The greenery due to these pine trees at the top of the valley drops the temperature by an average of 2.6 degrees centigrade in January. The highest point is the centre of attention for tourists to view the phenomenal charm of nature.

    About Neelum Valley

  • Location
    • Neelum (spelt also Neelam; Urdu: نیلم [niːləm]) is a district of Pakistan-administered Azad Kashmir in the disputed Kashmir region.[1] It is the northernmost of 10 districts located within the Pakistani-administered territory of Azad Kashmir. Taking up the larger part of the Neelam Valley, the district has a population of around 191,000 people (as of 2017).[3] It was among the worst-hit areas of Pakistan during the 2005 Kashmir earthquake.
